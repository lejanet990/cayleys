Cayley's Formula Proofs

Cayley's Formula tells us that the number of labeled trees on n vertices, or the number of spanning trees of a complete graph \(K_n\) is \(n^{n-2}\). The most common way to prove this is to use Kirchoff's matrix tree theorem, a more general theorem that allows us to find the total number of spanning trees of any arbitrary graph. However, more ingenious proofs by Prufer, Pitman, and Joyal.

Prufer's method involves finding a bijective mapping from a sequence of numbers to a spanning tree. Pitman's proof involves double-counting how many ways directed edges can be added to an empty graph of n vertices to form a rooted tree. Joyal's proof, the most recent of the three, involves finding a bijective mapping of a sequence to a doubly-rooted or vertebrate tree.
